## Languages and Meaning

I've never considered myself to be the brightest student in all the disciplines per se (some of them felt rather boring), but I could always find my passion and become fascinated with it, engaging fully, whatever it may be.

In times of my studying it was the language, or rather the art of conveying the meaning from one language into the other, from person to person, from culture to culture. On the surface it might feel rather straightforward — just find the same words in the target language and put them together — why bother in complications.

But on the inside it is all about the peculiarities of the context, the larger picture — the person, their background, cultural and educational peculiarities, family upbringing.

Digging deeper you'll find that all those tiny details have enormous meaning — the tone, the rhythm, the particular wording are all pieces of the puzzle that must be meticulously deciphered, recognized, and accounted for if you truly want to express the **MEANING**, to paint the same picture in the recipient's world. It's a hard and scrupulous but fascinating detective work if you please.

In live interpretation it's even more so — on top of everything already mentioned, you have to learn reading people — the tone, the look in the eye, the light smile in the corner of your face, the subtle body language, the posture, the laughs — all provide a more encompassing context to unpack a particular worldview and transfer it into another person's dimension. **THAT** is the real magic behind the curtain. **THAT** depth of experience is where I've found my passion at and that is what I've been and still am so fascinated with.

> Soon enough, I've started to realize that I carry that search for depth over to everything I truly love doing in my path.
